# Raja Ampat

## Status
**GOOD — hit in late 2024 but recovering strongly**

- First mass bleaching in central region Nov-Dec 2024. Cape Kri and Dampier Strait most affected (Alert Level 2).
- By March-April 2025, bleaching was already reversing. July 2025 marine biologist confirmed recovery, "fish density mostly stunning."
- The area covers 4.5 million hectares — remote outer reefs and upwelling zones barely affected.
- **Still one of the world's two or three best liveaboard destinations.** Fish diversity unmatched globally.

## Key Dive Sites

### Dampier Strait

#### Cape Kri
5–30m. Holds the world record fish count (374 species on a single dive). Reef slope with hard and soft corals swept by moderate-to-strong currents. Massive schools of fusiliers, barracuda, grey reef sharks, giant trevally, Napoleon wrasse.

#### Blue Magic
8–30m. Submerged seamount in Dampier Strait. Strong currents bring pelagic action — manta rays (seasonal), grey reef and whitetip reef sharks, schools of surgeonfish and trevally. Coral cover on the pinnacle top is good but secondary to the fish life.

#### Manta Sandy
10–18m. Shallow sandy-bottom cleaning station in Dampier Strait. Reef mantas visit reliably Oct–Apr; operators report 80–90% encounter success in season. Divers kneel on sand and watch mantas circle overhead.

#### Sardine Reef
5–25m. Named for resident clouds of silversides and sardines. Reef sharks, sweetlips, batfish, barracuda. Moderate currents.

### Fam Islands & Penemu

#### Melissa's Garden
3–25m. Fam Islands area with extensive table coral (Acropora) gardens — considered one of the most photogenic hard coral sites in the region. High reef fish density; hawksbill turtles common.

#### Boo Windows
5–25m. Rock island with swim-through arches covered in soft corals and sea fans. Wobbegong sharks resting under ledges, nudibranchs, scenic topography.

### Northern Raja Ampat

#### Wayag
5–20m. Iconic karst lagoon landscape of northern Raja Ampat. Reef sharks, coral gardens, and the famous above-water panoramic viewpoint. Requires longer-range liveaboard itinerary to reach.

#### Mike's Point
5–35m. Wall dive with sea fans and black coral. Pygmy seahorses (Bargibant's) found on gorgonians. Moderate current.

### Misool

#### Citrus Ridge (Misool)
5–30m. Southern Raja Ampat within the Misool marine reserve. Extremely high soft coral density — fans, whips, barrel sponges. Epaulette (walking) sharks on night dives over nearby shallow reef flats.

## Marine Life
- **Manta rays**: Reef mantas peak Oct–Apr at Manta Sandy, Manta Ridge, and Blue Magic. Highly reliable in season. Oceanic mantas occasionally at exposed southern sites but not reliable.
- **Sharks**: Grey reef (Cape Kri, Blue Magic), whitetip reef (widespread), blacktip reef (shallows). Tasselled wobbegong sharks are a Raja Ampat specialty — Boo Windows and overhang sites. Raja Ampat epaulette shark (endemic *Hemiscyllium* species) on night dives over shallow reef flats — reliable with knowledgeable guide.
- **Pygmy seahorses**: Bargibant's and Denise's on gorgonian fans at multiple sites. Guides know specific fans; sightings reliable.
- **Schooling fish**: Fusiliers, surgeonfish, jacks, barracuda, bumphead parrotfish in large aggregations. Cape Kri and Sardine Reef are standouts.
- **Pelagics**: Giant trevally, dogtooth tuna, occasional eagle rays. Whale sharks rare but reported in Cenderawasih Bay (see dedicated Triton Bay / Cenderawasih section — near-guaranteed whale shark encounters year-round at bagan platforms).
- **Population trends**: Fish biomass surveys by Conservation International and TNC show stable or increasing populations within the MPA since enforcement improved post-2012.

## Dive Conditions
- **Visibility**: 15–25m typical, occasionally 30m+ in southern Misool. Can drop to 10m in plankton-rich Dampier Strait (which is what attracts the mantas). Southern sites generally offer better visibility.
- **Temperature**: 27–30C year-round. Thermoclines can drop to 24C at depth. 3mm wetsuit standard; some prefer 5mm for multiple daily dives.
- **Currents**: Moderate to strong, especially in Dampier Strait. Blue Magic, Cape Kri, and Shotgun can have ripping currents (advanced level). Many sites manageable for intermediates with briefing and guide support.
- **Experience level**: Operators typically require Advanced Open Water and 50+ logged dives minimum.

## Season
- **Liveaboard season**: October–April (some operators extend into May or start in September). June–August off-season due to southeast monsoon (rougher seas, limited availability).
- **Best for mantas**: November–March at Dampier Strait sites.
- **Best visibility**: October–November and March–April.
- **Calmest seas**: November–early January, between monsoon transitions.

## Practical Info
- **Access**: Fly to Sorong (SOQ) via Jakarta or Makassar. Garuda, Lion Air, and Batik Air operate connections. Some operators include a hotel night in Sorong.
- **Liveaboard operators**: Damai, Dewi Nusantara, Indo Siren, Coralia, Samambaia, Gaia Love, Papua Explorer (mid-range), plus several Indonesian phinisi-style vessels.
- **Itineraries**: Northern routes (Dampier Strait, Wayag, Kawe), central routes (Dampier Strait, Fam/Penemu), southern routes (Misool marine reserve). 10–12 night trips can combine north and south; 7-night trips typically cover one zone.
- **Marine park entry fee**: ~IDR 1,000,000 for foreign visitors (~USD 60–65), valid one year. Verify current amount before travel.
- **Recompression chamber**: Nearest in Manado or Makassar — both multi-hour flights from Sorong. DAN insurance strongly recommended.

## Conservation
- **Raja Ampat MPA network**: ~2 million hectares across multiple marine protected areas, established progressively from 2007.
- **Misool Foundation**: 1,220 km² no-take zone around Misool. Documented significant shark and fish population recovery since enforcement began. Reef restoration and community education programs.
- **SEA People**: Coral reef monitoring and restoration using reef stars (metal structures seeded with coral fragments).
- **Patrol enforcement**: Community-based patrol system has expanded significantly. Blast fishing and cyanide fishing dramatically reduced in patrolled zones, though some remote areas remain vulnerable.
- **Shark sanctuary**: Shark fishing banned throughout Raja Ampat since 2012. Commercial fishing banned within core MPA zones. Traditional community fishing permitted in designated zones with gear restrictions.

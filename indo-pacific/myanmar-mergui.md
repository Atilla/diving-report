# Myanmar — Mergui (Myeik) Archipelago

## Status

- Over 800 islands, many unexplored. One of the last frontier diving areas in Southeast Asia. Historically remote with very low tourism and fishing pressure.
- **Scientific monitoring has collapsed post-coup (Feb 2021)**. International research collaborations suspended, in-country marine scientists displaced. Most information comes from liveaboard operator observations rather than systematic surveys.
- The archipelago sits in the same thermal regime as the northern [Thailand — Andaman North](thailand-andaman-north.md) Similans — comparable 2024 bleaching stress expected. Shallow reefs likely impacted, but deeper structures and current-swept sites probably in reasonable condition. Some operators reported conditions "better than expected" compared to the Similans.

## Key Dive Sites

#### Black Rock

Premier manta site, strong currents, good condition. Appeal is pelagic/current-based.

#### Western Rocky

Dramatic topography (tunnels, chimneys, arches), excellent soft corals, whitetip reef sharks, nurse sharks, outstanding macro life. Good to excellent condition.

#### Shark Cave

Resident nurse sharks and whitetips in cavern systems. Less affected by bleaching.

#### North Twin / South Twin

Mixed coral coverage — excellent hard coral gardens in places, some past bleaching damage. Good macro on boulder fields.

#### Burma Banks

Submerged seamounts famous for silvertip sharks. Trip reports 2023-2025 are mixed — some good encounters, others note reduced shark numbers vs 10-15 years ago. Fishing pressure (longlining, shark finning) is the concern here, not coral.

## Marine Life

- Mantas at Black Rock (Feb-Apr best). Whitetip and blacktip reef sharks common. Nurse sharks at multiple sites. Occasional whale sharks and leopard sharks.
- **Macro life is a particular strength** — widely regarded as superior to Thai sites. Ornate ghost pipefish, harlequin shrimp, Coleman's shrimp, nudibranchs, frogfish, seahorses.
- Some divers note reduced fish biomass at certain sites, possibly reflecting increased fishing pressure during reduced tourism/enforcement.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 10-40m depending on season and site. Best Feb-Apr (20-40m). Plankton blooms Feb-mid-Apr reduce viz to 10-15m at some sites but increase whale shark and manta probability.
- **Temperature**: 24-28C. Coolest in Dec (~26C), warmest by Apr-May (~28-30C).
- **Currents**: Strong at Black Rock and outer sites. Variable elsewhere.

## Season

- **Liveaboard season:** Nov-Apr only (SW monsoon closes operations May-Oct — rough seas, poor visibility, unsafe transits to outer sites).
- **Best:** Feb-Apr. Warmest water, calmest seas, best visibility (20-40m). Plankton blooms Feb-mid-Apr reduce viz to 10-15m at some sites but increase whale shark and manta probability.
- **Early season (Nov-Dec):** cooler water, excellent visibility, fewer boats. Good conditions but slightly lower pelagic activity.
- **Mantas at Black Rock:** Feb-Apr peak.
- **Macro:** stable year-round within the dive season — muck/macro critters are not seasonal.
- **Wetsuit:** 3mm sufficient most of the season; 5mm for early season (Dec) or thermocline-sensitive divers.

## Practical Info

- **Trips are running** but at reduced volume. Thailand-based operators (Ranong/Khao Lak) with border crossing at Kawthaung. Permits functional but unpredictable — last-minute changes possible.
- **Safety**: most Western governments maintain "do not travel" advisories for Myanmar. Insurance coverage must be explicitly verified. Nearest recompression chamber is in Phuket (many hours away from remote sites). No cell coverage in outer archipelago.
- **Ethical dimension**: permit fees reach the military government. Personal decision for each diver.
- **Illegal fishing**: enforcement has collapsed post-coup. Reports of blast fishing evidence, increased fishing boats, and declining shark numbers at Burma Banks.
- **For the right diver**: an unmatched frontier experience with outstanding macro, genuine exploration feel, and far fewer divers than anywhere in Thailand. But requires higher risk tolerance and flexibility.

# Philippines (Tubbataha, Apo Reef, Visayas)

## Status

- Tubbataha: moderate bleaching in shallow areas (May 2024), but not spatially extensive overall. UNESCO outlook: "good with some concerns." Remote location = fast recovery.
- **Eastern Visayas "super reefs"** (Panaon Island): corals survived ALL recent bleaching events. Scientifically documented climate refugia (50 Reefs Study; Oceana expedition found 60% cover, 3x national average). Panaon Island Protected Seascape (61,204 hectares) signed into law August 29, 2025.
- Bohol Sea, Palawan, Visayan Sea identified as potential climate refugia.
- **Tubbataha remains one of the world's best liveaboard destinations.** Season: mid-March to mid-June.
- The Philippines sits at the apex of the Coral Triangle — over 500 coral species and 2,500+ reef fish species recorded.

## Key Dive Sites

### Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park

#### Amos Rock

Seamount rising from deep water to ~8m. Strong currents attract grey reef sharks, whitetips, mantas (seasonal), large schools of jacks and barracuda. Advanced.

#### Shark Airport

Broad shallow reef plateau (5-14m) where whitetip reef sharks rest on sandy bottom. Excellent hard coral cover historically. Good wide-angle photography. Moderate currents.

#### Wall Street

Dramatic vertical wall dropping from ~5m to beyond 60m on the North Atoll. Dense soft coral coverage, gorgonian fans, reef fish aggregations. Reliable shark sightings along wall edge.

#### Delsan Wreck

Cargo vessel remains on South Atoll reef flat (3-8m). Encrusted with coral growth, hawksbill turtles frequent the area. Easy dive, good for later dives of the day.

#### Black Rock

South Atoll pinnacle. Hammerhead sightings (not guaranteed), mantas, dense schools of surgeonfish and fusiliers. Intermediate to advanced.

#### Bird Islet

Shallow reef surrounding the seabird nesting island on North Atoll. Large green and hawksbill turtle population. Pristine shallow hard coral gardens.

#### Washing Machine

Named for unpredictable swirling currents. Pelagic-rich site; experienced divers only.

### Tubbataha Practical Info

- **Season**: Strictly mid-March to mid-June (park closed outside this window due to monsoons).
- **Departure port**: Puerto Princesa, Palawan (~10-12 hours sailing).
- **Trip length**: Typically 6 days / 7 nights.
- **Liveaboards**: M/Y Atlantis Azores, M/Y Resolute, S/Y Philippine Siren, M/V Stella Maris Explorer, among others (16-22 guests).
- **Park fee**: ~$100 USD conservation fee per diver (verify with Tubbataha Management Office).
- **Recompression chamber**: None on-site. Nearest in Puerto Princesa and Cebu. DAN membership strongly advised.
- **Experience level**: Most operators require 50+ logged dives; Advanced Open Water recommended due to strong currents at several sites.
- **Conditions**: Visibility 20-40m (often 30m+), temperature 27-30C, moderate to strong currents.

### Apo Reef Natural Park

The Philippines' largest contiguous reef system and the second-largest in the world (off Mindoro — not to be confused with Apo Island near Dumaguete).

Whitetip and blacktip reef sharks, occasional hammerheads, sea turtles, Napoleon wrasse, large schools of jacks. Manta sightings sporadic.

Visibility 15-30m; currents moderate to strong on outer walls. Best season Nov-May.

Some Tubbataha-bound liveaboards incorporate Apo Reef as a stopover. Also reachable by day-trip bangka from Sablayan, Mindoro.

Protected area but enforcement historically less rigorous than Tubbataha. Coral condition mixed — some areas excellent, others show historical blast-fishing damage.

### Visayas

#### Malapascua — Monad Shoal

Submerged plateau at 16-25m. **The only known site in the world offering reliable, near-daily thresher shark encounters** at cleaning stations, primarily at dawn (5:30-6:00 AM). Year-round but best Nov-May. Mantas seasonal. Visayas liveaboard itineraries (e.g. Philippine Siren) include this as a key stop.

#### Moalboal (Cebu)

Massive resident sardine school (Sardinella) at Panagsama reef, present year-round since ~2014. Snorkeling depth, comparable density to South Africa's sardine run but reliably stationary. Nearby Pescador Island offers walls, caverns, whitetip sharks.

#### Dumaguete / Dauin

World-class muck diving on black volcanic sand — blue-ringed octopus, mimic octopus, frogfish (multiple species), flamboyant cuttlefish, wonderpus, extraordinary nudibranch diversity, pygmy seahorses. Apo Island (20 min from Dauin) is a globally cited community MPA success — excellent hard coral, turtle cleaning stations, schools of jacks.

#### Oslob Whale Sharks

Hand-feeding operation attracting whale sharks daily. Controversial — concerns about behavioral modification, disruption of natural migration, and crowding. Most serious dive operators and liveaboards do not include it in itineraries.

#### Visayas Liveaboard Routes

Typically 7-10 nights connecting Malapascua, Leyte/Southern Leyte (whale sharks at Sogod Bay — non-feeding encounters), Bohol (Balicasag Island walls), Dumaguete/Dauin, and Moalboal.

## Marine Life

- **Thresher sharks**: Monad Shoal — only reliable daily encounters globally.
- **Grey reef sharks**: Tubbataha — on every dive.
- **Whale sharks**: Sogod Bay (non-feeding encounters), Oslob (controversial feeding), Apo Reef (sporadic).
- **Turtles**: Green and hawksbill widespread, especially at Tubbataha and Apo Island.
- **Muck/macro**: Dauin world-class — blue-ringed octopus, mimic octopus, frogfish, flamboyant cuttlefish, pygmy seahorses.
- **Sardine school**: Moalboal — year-round resident school at snorkeling depth.
- **Coral Triangle apex**: 500+ coral species, 2,500+ reef fish species.

Northeast monsoon (Amihan, Nov-Feb) brings drier conditions and better visibility to western-facing sites. Southwest monsoon (Habagat, Jun-Oct) can reduce visibility and create rougher seas.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: International flights to Manila or Cebu. Puerto Princesa for Tubbataha. Domestic connections throughout the Visayas.
- **Liveaboards**: M/Y Atlantis Azores, M/Y Resolute, S/Y Philippine Siren, M/V Stella Maris Explorer (Tubbataha). Philippine Siren also runs Visayas itineraries.
- **Tubbataha park fee**: ~$100 USD conservation fee per diver.
- **Recompression chambers**: Puerto Princesa and Cebu (for Tubbataha); various locations in the Visayas.
- **Experience level**: Tubbataha requires 50+ dives, Advanced OW. Visayas muck diving accessible to Open Water. Monad Shoal suitable for AOW.

## Conservation

- **Tubbataha Management Office**: Co-managed by national government and City of Puerto Princesa. Ranger stations on both atolls year-round. Strict enforcement: no anchoring (moorings only), no extraction. One of the most effective MPAs in Southeast Asia.
- **LAMAVE (Large Marine Vertebrates Research Institute Philippines)**: Photo-ID and acoustic tagging research on whale sharks, mantas, and thresher sharks. Citizen science programs allow divers to contribute sighting data.
- **Community MPAs**: The Philippines pioneered community-based marine management. Apo Island's MPA (est. 1982) is a globally cited success — fish biomass inside the reserve is multiple times higher than outside. Hundreds of community MPAs across the Visayas, though enforcement quality varies.
- **Challenges**: Dynamite and cyanide fishing reduced but not eliminated in some areas. Climate-driven bleaching is beyond local management control.
